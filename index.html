Cause-by-Cause Defense Against Demurrer

For each cause of action, the demurrer likely argues failure to state facts sufficient to constitute a cause of action (CCP §430.10(e)). Below is the opposition strategy for each.

#Cause of ActionLikely Demurrer ArgumentOpposition StrategyStrength
1 Breach of Contract Lease terms not specifically pled; no privity with all defendants Cite lease agreement (March 2021), deposit receipt ($9,000), partial refund ($1,500 on 1/19/25). All defendants acted as agents per ¶7 of complaint. Attach lease as exhibit. STRONG
2 CC §1950.5 Violation Statutory requirements not met; itemized deductions may have been provided 21-day rule violation is per se bad faith. Only $1,500 returned with no itemized statement. Demand 2x penalty ($15,000) per §1950.5(l). This is the strongest claim — clear statutory violation. STRONG
3 Fraud (Concealment) Insufficient specificity; no duty to disclose; no reliance pled Defendants concealed Peter’s deposit claim from escrow/buyer during April 2025 sale. Property sold for $375,000+ while $7,500 deposit outstanding. Concealment of material facts during real estate transaction creates duty. Cite Bigler-Engler v. Breg for concealment elements. STRONG
4 Constructive Trust & Equitable Lien Equitable remedy not properly pled; no specific property identified Sale proceeds from April 2025 transaction are identifiable. Defendants were unjustly enriched by retaining deposit while profiting from sale. Request court trace funds. May need to amend for more specificity on proceeds tracing. MODERATE
5 Conversion Deposit was not specific, identifiable property; landlord had right to deduct $9,000 deposit is identifiable personal property held in trust per §1950.5. Defendants exercised unauthorized dominion by using funds for cosmetic renovations to enhance sale value (¶13). No lawful deductions documented. STRONG
6 Breach of Fiduciary Duty No fiduciary relationship existed with all defendants; Allen Oh unlicensed Youngse Kwon is a licensed agent under New Star Realty (¶5-6). Fiduciary duty arises from broker-tenant relationship in deposit handling. Allen Oh acted as de facto agent in violation of B&P §10131. Their conduct breached trust fund obligations. MODERATE
